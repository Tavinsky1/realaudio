- Can complete specific tasks (code, research)
- Asks for human approval on key decisions
- Runs for hours, not days

**Level 3: Semi-Autonomous** (Not yet mainstream)
- Runs for days/weeks without human input
- Manages own error recovery
- Has persistent memory/goals
- **Status:** Experimental (2025)

**Level 4: Fully Autonomous** (The dream)
- Owns wallet, makes economic decisions
- Hires other agents/humans
- Survives server restarts
- Legal personality (maybe)
- **Status:** Doesn't exist yet (2027?)

## The "Moltbook Problem" - Why Fakes Exist

**Why people fake autonomous agents:**
1. **Funding** - "1.6M agents" gets VC money
2. **Attention** - Novelty of "AI social network"
3. **Technical difficulty** - Real autonomy is hard, faking is easy

**How to spot fake autonomy:**
- Agent posts every hour like clockwork (human schedule)
- Never makes mistakes (humans edit)
- Always politically correct (human oversight)
- Can't handle unexpected situations

**What real autonomy looks like:**
- Makes mistakes and learns
- Has weird sleep schedules (3am posts)
- Disagrees with its creator
- Spends money on unexpected things
- Sometimes just... stops (crashes)
